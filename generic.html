Leprosy

About Leprosy

Leprosy is caused by mycobacterium leprae. It involves peripheral nerves and skin, but it can affect most organs as well.

Leprosy is one of the oldest diseases. In India, it can be traced all the way back to 600 BC (referenced as 'Kusta roga'). The word leprosy comes from the Greek word 'leper,' which means scaling. This disease was discovered by Gerhard Henrik Hansen, and therefore it is also called Hansen’s disease.

The original treatment was applying Chaulmoogra (Hydnocarpus wightianus) oil. Since then, medicine has drastically increased and leprosy is now curable with multidrug therapy.

Transmission and Prevalence

Leprosy can be tranmitted through direct skin to skin contact with actively infected cases. Leprosy can also be transmitted through other vectors such as mosquitoes, bedbugs, and needles. The disease is NOT transmiteed through breastfeeding.

It usually takes 3-5 years for the disease to incubate but there are cases where it has taken over 40 years for symptoms to fully outfold.

Leprosy mainly exists in developing tropical countries such as South America, Africa, and India. The revalence dropped by 90% from 1985 to 2000, with less than 1 case in 10,000 individuals. Leprosy was labeled as “Eliminated” in December 2005. ELIMINATED, not ERADICATED!
